Wireframe Index & Guide · Round 2

Index Special Offers — Quick Create

Round 2 keeps round 1's confirmed direction — a one-page Create New Offer surface, no stepper — and rebuilds the parts the review flagged. The headline fix: the price formula is no longer "one index + a product." It's the real model — choose a formula template (three variables, seven columns each), then edit the variable lines straight in the table, with the resulting price and differential above it.

Overview

These five static HTML wireframes iterate on round 1 for the Index Special Offers quick-create flow, built from the vendored Gravitate WireframeDesignSystem (wf- components, --wf- tokens; lo-fi scaffolding isolated under lf-). Round 1 proved the one-page direction and the production "Create New Offer" chrome; the review confirmed both and concentrated feedback on what lives inside the page — chiefly the price formula and the invite step.

The review in one line

"We want all the sections on one page, no scroll — yes. The price formula is the bulk of what we need to keep thinking through." Everything in round 2 follows from getting the formula right and making invite scale.

Changes from Round 1

  • Price formula rebuilt (the big one). R1 reduced pricing to Index + Index product + Differential — which the review called "just not correct." R2 models a formula as three variables, each with seven columns (name, publisher, instrument, type, date rule, operator, weight). You don't author from scratch; you choose a formula template (from a bottom-sheet chooser, disabled until product & location are set), then edit the variable lines straight in the table. The resulting price (editable) and formula differential sit on separate lines above the table. See screen 03.
  • Flow reordered. The first selection is now Product & location → Volume (the review's "what am I selling, how much"), which then drives which formulas you see. The R1 "Start / Template" row at the top is deferred.
  • Invite is now progressive & intent-based. R1 assumed everyone was grouped. R2 adds a mode switch — Groups (with pinning + free-text search for 25+ groups), Authorized to lift, Credit (ranked), and an All customers search fallback. See screen 04.
  • Entry = New → Fast or Full. The launchpad menu now mirrors Reece's words ("new fast or new full") instead of "Quick create / Full setup," and the fast page keeps a Switch to full escape hatch.
  • "Template" renamed to "Preset." The word was overloaded (a real template sets offer/auction + fixed/index). The saved-offer-configuration — "my good stuff, the way I do business" — is now a Preset, kept distinct from create-from-prior. Note this is distinct from formula templates — the price-formula building blocks keep their real product name ("Formula Template Selector"); only the offer-level saved config is renamed to Preset.
  • Timing custom wired to the day-deal picker. Presets stay; "Custom" reuses Rebecca & Abdul's day-deal "now / +1 / +2 / +6" date-time picker.
  • R1's live click-budget meter is gone. It was already dropped late in R1 when the rail became the customer preview; R2 doesn't reintroduce it.
  • Mobile not re-cut. R1 had a mobile screen; R2 holds it until the desktop formula/invite patterns settle (noted as open).

Shared layout structure

Carried forward from round 1. The create screens (02, 03, 04) use the production Create New Offer surface — a full-screen takeover, no app sidebar:

  • Top app bar (lf-topbar) — × close + bold "Create New Offer" title; a small dashed "Round 2 index" reviewer link at the right.
  • "Back to Offers" link (lf-backtolist) above the content.
  • Wide left content card (wf-card) holding the offer; groups separated by hairlines (lf-group), not nested wf-form-section cards.
  • Right "What customers will see" preview (lf-preview) on the hero — the four labelled sections (Product & Location, Pricing Strategy, Timing Windows, Target Customers).
  • Footer action bar (lf-offer-footer) — Cancel · reuse/▸full note · Send offer.
  • Bottom-sheet template chooser — the Formula Template Selector shown via lf-bottomsheet (≈85vh) over a dimmed page for the static "choose template" state.

Screens 01 (offers list) and 05 (sent) keep the app shell (wf-page-with-sidebar) since they aren't the create surface; both gain a Presets nav item.

Color & status language

Unchanged from round 1 — status colors mean status, primary blue means interactive intent / current selection, everything else neutral.

  • Primary blue — Send CTA, selected chip / tab (wf-tab-active), selected formula card (wf-card-selected), pinned-group pin, and the left edge of the formula readout & key preview section.
  • Success green — real status only: wf-badge-success "Live" / "Active", the sent toast.
  • Warning amber — "Closing" badge; a stretched credit utilization (86%).
  • Error red — reserved for validation; not used decoratively this round.
  • Monospace (wf-mono) — formula variable cells, differentials, volumes, credit figures.

03-price-formula.html

— Formula Template Selector (bottom sheet)Open wireframe →

Screen purpose

The template chooser, modelled on the production FormulaTemplateSelector in the prototype. It opens as a bottom-sheet drawer (≈85vh) over the dimmed Create New Offer page when you choose a template from screen 02.

Structure (mirrors the real component)

Header: an "Effective …" eyebrow, the title Formula Template Selector, the subtitle "Select a pre-built formula template…," and right-aligned Manage Formula Templates / Exit Templates. A filters bar with a Cards/List segmented toggle plus Search, Category, Products, Locations, Price Type. Then a horizontal scroll of template cards — each card shows the template name, Category / Products / Location metadata, a "Select Components — N selected" count, the component lines (e.g. "50% + OPIS · G3 Suboctane Gross / Prior day · Low"), a "Display Name" formula-preview box, and a Select Template button. A "+ Build a new formula" card ends the row.

Match the real surface

Choosing a template loads its variable lines into the editable table on screen 02, where the seller finishes filling them out. There is no separate edit drawer — the table itself is the editor. The chooser visually echoes the existing prototype component so it reads as the same product.

— Invite: progressive modesOpen wireframe →

Makes invite scale past the small-group case. A pill-tab mode switch; all four modes are shown stacked for review (in product the tab swaps the panel).

05-review-send.html

— Offer sentOpen wireframe →

Screen purpose

The payoff, plus the place that resolves the Preset-vs-prior question. A success toast, the created offer's detail card (now showing the real formula name + differential, invoices at lift, and the invite method used), and a "Reuse this" panel.

Reuse panel

Design decisions reflected

  1. A price formula is variables, not a single index. Three variable lines × seven columns (%, Publisher, Instrument, Type, Diff, Date Rule, Display), surfaced as an editable table loaded from a chosen template.
  2. Choose a template, then edit in the table. The quick path never authors from scratch; it picks a formula template (bottom-sheet chooser) and edits the variables inline — no separate edit drawer. The resulting price and differential sit above the table so they're never pushed down.
  3. Product drives the formula set. Choosing product/location first filters which formulas are offered — hence the reordered flow.
  4. Invite is a progressive choice of abstraction. Groups / Authorized / Credit / All, because not every seller invites by group and "fast" depends on the mental model.
  5. Pinning + search keep groups fast at scale. Chips alone break down past ~25 groups.
  6. Entry is Fast vs Full, with a live escape hatch. The fast page can always switch to the full wizard mid-flight.
  7. "Preset" replaces "template," and is distinct from create-from-prior. Preset = deliberate/repeated; prior = one-off recall; they feed each other.
  8. Reuse existing components. Day-deal time picker for custom timing; the existing Formula Template Selector (bottom sheet + cards); card/list pickers — no new patterns invented.
  9. Carry the one-page Create New Offer chrome forward. The confirmed R1 direction is unchanged; only the contents deepened.

Open for round 3

  1. Naming: confirm Preset (vs Playbook / Starting point) for the saved-offer configuration.
  2. Preset vs prior: keep both as designed, or collapse one into the other? (Current lean: keep both.)
  3. Formula Template Selector: confirm the existing production chooser can be reused as-is here, and how "Build a new formula" differs from picking + editing a template.
  4. Invite mode: explicit switch (as shown) or inferred from behavior? What is each seller's default mode, and can modes be combined (e.g. authorized and credit)?
  5. Variants for customer feedback: Reece asked for a couple of versions of the formula step to test — do we branch options in R3?
  6. Mobile: re-cut the flow for mobile once the formula + invite patterns are settled.
