Threading & background work

Scout is multi-threaded: every row — and the whole-view stream — can hold its own conversation that keeps working in the background while the analyst does other things. Each conversation moves through a small, well-defined state machine, and the moment any one of them finishes, a coordinated set of surfaces lights up together so the answer is impossible to miss.

Three thread states

A conversation is always in exactly one of three states. State drives every status affordance described below.

StateMeaningHow it reads
in-progressScout is still thinking on this conversationAnimated dots, a quiet shimmer
readyAn answer is waiting but has not been openedSolid dot plus an accent rail; counted as unseen
viewedThe answer has been opened and seenMuted; no longer counted

An unseen count aggregates every conversation currently in the ready state. Opening a ready conversation moves it to viewed and decrements that count.

Simulated think time & the single clock

In the prototype, a question takes a fixed ~20 seconds to move from in-progress to ready — a stand-in for real model latency. One global clock drives every surface, so countdowns and status flips stay in lockstep no matter which page the analyst is on.

Prototype-only The fixed 20-second delay and the shared clock are demo scaffolding. A production build replaces them with real streaming and live status; the lockstep behavior is what matters, not the number.

Surfaces that flip together on ready

When a thread crosses into ready, all of these update in the same beat:

  • The per-row dot for that row lights and begins pulsing.
  • The Chats unseen badge increments.
  • The cross-page name tag reads Scout is ready.
  • A card appears in the Control Panel for that thread.

Chats inbox

The Chats tab lists conversations as question-led cards (see 1, 2). Each card shows the lead question, a one-line answer preview, a timestamp (for example Jun 1 · 4:41 PM), a status badge (animated dots while thinking, a solid dot with an accent rail when ready, muted once viewed), a star toggle, and a ⋯ menu (favorite, delete, save). The tab itself carries the unseen count badge (see 3), and a control to start a fresh conversation sits at the top (see 4). Clicking a card slides into that conversation.

Per-row ready indicator

A pinned left-gutter marker of three dots signals each row's background state (see 1): lit and pulsing when that row's answer is ready, a quiet shimmer while in-progress, and absent when the row has been viewed or has no conversation at all. Clicking the marker jumps straight into that row's conversation.
