Information architecture

Scout lives in a single panel that holds two top-level views, reached through a tab strip: Chats and Library. Everything the analyst does — asking, reviewing past conversations, and reusing saved questions — happens inside this one surface, so navigation never leaves the pricing grid behind.

The tab strip is the panel's primary wayfinding control (see 1). Chats is list-first: it opens onto the full set of conversations rather than a single thread. Each conversation appears as a card; selecting one slides into that conversation, and a back affordance labeled ‹ All Chats returns to the list. There is no separate inbox view — the Chats list is the inbox, carrying an unseen-count badge when background answers are ready.

The Library view holds reusable saved items: Prompt (one question) and Path (an ordered sequence of questions). It offers search, a starred-only filter, and an ownership filter spanning All, Yours, and Shared with you.

Scoping

Scoping is the cross-cutting concept: whatever the analyst selects in the grid — a row, a single cell, or a multi-cell selection — becomes the conversation's context, and Scout answers about that scope; with nothing in focus, it answers about the whole view.

Panel modes

The panel presents in one of two modes, switched by a single control. It can float as a draggable, resizable card docked top-right, or dock as a sidebar at full height, pushing the grid leftward to make room.
