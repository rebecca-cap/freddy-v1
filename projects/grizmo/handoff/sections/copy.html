Copy & lexicon

Every surface speaks with one consistent vocabulary. The terms below are canonical: use them verbatim in labels, headings, and instructional copy, and never substitute near-synonyms. The product is named Scout, and a single conversation is always a chat in user-facing copy — never a "thread," even though threads are the internal mental model for background work.

Lexicon

These are the load-bearing nouns and verbs of the product. Keep capitalization as shown when a term names a feature or an item type.

TermMeaning
ScoutThe product — the embedded AI pricing co-pilot.
chatA single conversation. The user-facing word for what is internally a thread; never say "thread" in copy.
Ask ScoutThe trigger action and toolbar label that opens the panel.
context / in focusThe current scope — the row, cell, or selection the answer is about.
PromptA saved item that is one reusable question.
PathA saved item that is an ordered sequence of questions.
RunThe verb for executing a Path's steps in sequence.
OpenThe verb for dropping a Prompt's text into a new ask.
confidenceThe High / Medium / Low rating every answer carries.
sourcesThe citable references behind an answer.
follow-upA suggested next question offered beneath an answer.
