Panel anatomy & layout

The panel is a single vertical stack of five regions, ordered top to bottom, that stays consistent whether Scout floats as a card or docks as a full-height sidebar. Everything an analyst can do to Scout itself lives in these regions; a handful of supporting surfaces deliberately render outside the panel.

Top-to-bottom chrome

  1. Title row — the mascot mark and Scout wordmark sit on the left; a control cluster sits on the right, in order: Save as path, Clear, Mode (float/dock), Minimize, and Close. Save-as-path and Clear appear greyed and inert when there is nothing to act on (see 2, 3). In floating mode this whole row doubles as the drag handle (see 1).
  2. View nav — the Chats / Library tab strip. The Chats tab carries an unseen-count badge when background answers are ready. Inside an open conversation, a ‹ All Chats back control replaces the tab strip.
  3. Context-chips bar (Chats only) — the current scope shown as removable pills, or, when empty, the hint Nothing in focus · click a cell alongside a + Add a cell affordance.
  4. Body — the active view: welcome, conversation, chats list, or library. It slides horizontally when switching views, the push/pop direction matching the nav direction.
  5. Composer footer (Chats only) — a multi-line input that grows with content, placeholder Ask Scout about your quote book…, a Send button (inert while empty or busy), and a circular Undo affordance to the left that reverses the last turn.

Geometry & behavior

In floating mode the panel anchors top-right with a margin and auto-fits to its content height, growing until it reaches a cap of roughly 80vh, after which the body scrolls and the chrome stays put. A resize grip at the bottom-right adjusts height only — width is fixed in floating mode and the cursor reads as a vertical-resize. Dragging the title row repositions the floating card; that position persists across a minimize and resets only on discard.

The Mode control toggles between floating and docked. Docked, the panel becomes a full-height sidebar that pushes the grid left rather than overlaying it (see 1).

Surfaces that render outside the panel

Several elements live beyond the panel frame and persist as the analyst works:

  • the Ask Scout trigger and its first-run intro bubble
  • the minimized mini-bubble (Resume conversation)
  • grid cell highlighting and the per-row ready dots
  • the cross-page name tag and the Control-Panel alert cards
  • toasts
  • the bottom prototype-timer bar
One ring, always on An animated shimmer border rings the panel in both modes; it signals "a thinking machine" and is detailed under Motion.
