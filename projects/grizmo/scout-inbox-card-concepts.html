Scout — Chat Inbox Card Concepts

Three ways to identify a chat in the inbox, without borrowing the messaging-app "sender title" metaphor that doesn't fit. Lo-fi wireframes using the real Scout palette so you can judge them side by side.

The problem. The card today leads with a bold title in the slot a messaging app uses for the sender ("Sarah", "Mom"). But a Scout chat has no sender — it's your own question to an assistant, optionally scoped to a row. So the title falls back to "New chat", which reads as a status and collides with the Viewed badge.

The real question: "What is this chat about, and what's its state?" The strongest identifier is the question you asked; the useful secondary is the data scope (which row/book). Status + time are constant — they just get placed differently in each option below.

Three options

A · Question-led

Drop the title slot entirely. The question is the headline; the answer is the preview; scope rides as a small chip. The truest "what is this about."

My read

Lean A (Question-led) as the default. A chat's identity is the question — leading with it gives the best scent and kills the awkward "New chat" title for good. Keep the scope as a small chip so row-scoped chats still announce their data.

If row-scoped chats dominate in practice, B organizes better. C is the move only if the inbox routinely gets long. All three reserve "New chat" for genuinely empty sessions and keep the Ready state you already like (white + purple rail + dot).
