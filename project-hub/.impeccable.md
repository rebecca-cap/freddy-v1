## Design Context

### Users
Frank Overland (AI product designer at Gravitate Energy) and a small set of internal Gravitate designers / PMs. Used at a desk during the workday to manage prototype branches against the stripped-down Gravitate frontend ("proto-base"). The tool is internal-only, single-tenant, low-traffic — closer to a power-user control panel than a customer SaaS app.

### Brand Personality
Gravitate is a commodity / energy trading platform. Three words for this Hub: **refined, deliberate, made-by-someone-who-cares**. Should feel like a craftsman's workshop, not a consumer dashboard. Frank's own taste leans toward considered minimalism with personality, not maximalist.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Internal tool, but designed *with* taste — Frank looks at it every day and uses it to ship work.
- Avoid generic shadcn black-and-white (the current state). Avoid AI palette tells (cyan-on-dark, purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, side-stripe borders).
- Color story should hint at the energy / refining / industrial world without being literal (no oil-drop icons, no pipeline imagery).
- Both light and dark are viable; default leans warm dark because Frank works long hours and the existing chrome already biases gray.
- Typographic personality matters — pair a distinctive display face with a sturdy workhorse body.

### Design Principles
1. **Quiet maximalism** — restrained surfaces, but every detail (typography, spacing, accent color) has a point of view.
2. **Warmth over gloss** — tinted neutrals, not gray. No glassmorphism, no glow.
3. **One accent, used sparingly** — 60/30/10 weight, never decorative gradients.
4. **Editorial spacing rhythm** — vary spacing for hierarchy; don't uniformly pad.
5. **Numbers and metadata feel intentional** — mono used for ports, branch names, timestamps; never as a lazy "developer" shorthand.
